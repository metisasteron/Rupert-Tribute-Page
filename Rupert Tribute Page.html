Prince Rupert of the Rhine

Prince Rupert is a fascinating figure in a fascinating time. From his childhood, adventure swirled around him. He was a child of a King who lost his kingdom and the Nephew of a King who lost his head. He was an accomplished military officer, artist, and scientist. He also spent time in piracy.

Let's take a look at the life of the Prince.

Timeline

  • 1619 - Rupert was born in Prague, Bohemia, to Frederick V of the Palatinate and Elizabeth, the daughter of Charles I of England.
  • 1620 - Frederick V was defeated at the Battle of White Mountain, the first battle of the Thirty Years War. Frederick and his family moved to the Hague in the Netherlands.
  • 1632 - Rupert's father died returning from a meeting with Gustav II Adolph of Sweden, a key ally. Gustav II died a month later at the Battle of LÃ¼tzen. Rupert and his siblings are sent to live in England with their uncle.
  • 1635-1638 - Rupert fought in several campaigns in the Thirty Years War.
  • 1638-1641 - After defeat at the Battle of Vlotho, Rupert was taken captive by Imperial Forces. His captivity was not harsh. He had an affair with his jailer's daughter, and he received his famous dog, Boy.
  • 1642-1646 - Rupert served as General of Horse in the Royalist army during the First English Civil War. He was involved in many important victories, but he also led the Royalist army at the critical defeat at Marston Moor. He was banished from England by Parliamentarians after the Siege of Oxford.
